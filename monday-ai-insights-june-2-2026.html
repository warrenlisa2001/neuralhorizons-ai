01 — Capital & Valuation
✓ Confirmed: Week of 26 May 2026

Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI — and the Valuation Race Is Moving Faster Than Anyone Expected

The round closed during the week of 26 May. Sequoia Capital, Dragoneer Investment Group, Altimeter Capital, and Greenoaks Capital Partners co-led, each contributing approximately $2 billion. Peter Thiel's Founders Fund, General Catalyst, Microsoft, and Nvidia also participated. The total exceeded $30 billion at a pre-money valuation above $900 billion — vaulting Anthropic past OpenAI's $852 billion March valuation to become the world's most valuable private AI startup.

The revenue story behind the valuation is real. Anthropic's annualised run rate rose from $9 billion at end-2025 to approximately $30 billion by early April 2026. Enterprise customers now represent around 80% of revenue, with more than 1,000 businesses spending over $1 million annually. Claude Code drove a significant part of that growth — which makes the news that Microsoft cancelled most of its Claude Code licences last week especially worth sitting with.

⚠ Both Sides · The Microsoft signal The Verge reported last week that Microsoft cancelled most of its Claude Code licences — redirecting engineers to GitHub Copilot CLI — after discovering per-engineer token bills of $500 to $2,000 per month. Uber also confirmed it burned through its entire 2026 AI budget in four months with no clear line to consumer outcomes. Anthropic's revenue growth is real. So is the cost reckoning happening inside its enterprise customers right now.

For MENA leaders, the valuation race has a direct regional dimension. Abu Dhabi's MGX participated in OpenAI's March round. Gulf sovereign capital is now embedded in the most valuable AI companies in the world — which means the region's financial exposure to AI's capital war is substantial, and its strategic interest in which AI platforms dominate is not neutral.

02 — The Honest Reckoning
✓ Confirmed: 26 May 2026

Sam Altman Said He Was "Pretty Wrong." Dario Amodei Said the Same. Neither Cancelled the Forecast.

On 26 May, speaking at a Commonwealth Bank of Australia conference in Sydney, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman told the audience he was "pretty wrong" about AI's economic impact on jobs. He had expected more entry-level white-collar displacement by now. He was "delighted to be wrong." Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei, who once predicted AI could eliminate 50% of white-collar jobs, said the same week that automation may actually expand the work people do. Fortune labelled it a coordinated walk-back.

The timing: OpenAI filed its S-1 confidentially with the SEC on 22 May. Altman's reversal came four days later. Anthropic is eyeing its own public listing later this year. Calling this a coincidence requires more faith than the evidence supports.

⬡ Seeing Both Sides Clearly

What is true
Yale Budget Lab data through March 2026 shows no meaningful change in unemployment for workers in high AI-exposure occupations. The apocalypse, so far, has not materialised on the timeline either CEO predicted. AI is replacing tasks, not yet entire jobs at scale.
What is also true
Meta laid off 8,000 employees last week and cited AI. A Mercer survey of 825 C-level executives found 99% expect AI to reduce headcount in the next two years. Agentic AI — AI that acts, not just advises — has only just launched. The labour data on autonomous AI agents does not yet exist, because the products are weeks old.

For leaders in the UAE and MENA: the honest read is that the disruption is coming, but slower and more uneven than the original framing suggested. That does not mean the urgency has gone. It means the window to build AI capability into your organisation — while the labour market is still absorbing the early wave — is more valuable than it has ever been. The organisations using this period to embed AI into operations will be setting the baseline that everyone else has to match when the curve steepens.

⬡ The signal for this region The UAE's 70.1% AI adoption rate means the tools are in the hands of the workforce. What the reversal from Altman and Amodei confirms is that adoption alone does not create economic transformation — it creates the precondition for it. The question for every MENA business leader right now is not "are we using AI?" It is "what are we actually changing?"
03 — The Price War
✓ Confirmed: 22–24 May 2026

DeepSeek Made the 75% Price Cut Permanent. This Is Not a Promotion. It Is a Structural Shift.

On 22 May, DeepSeek announced that its 75% promotional discount on V4-Pro — originally scheduled to expire 31 May — would not roll back. The promotional rate is now the permanent list price. V4-Pro now costs $0.435 per million input tokens and $0.87 per million output tokens. For context: OpenAI's GPT-5 charges $2.50 input and $10 output. Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 is $5 input and $25 output. DeepSeek V4-Pro is now roughly 11x cheaper than GPT-5 on input tokens.

DeepSeek runs on Huawei's Ascend 950 chips — which means its cost structure does not depend on Nvidia. The Ascend 950 supernode rollout expected in H2 2026 suggests further price cuts are structurally probable. DeepSeek does not need every API call to generate venture-scale margin. OpenAI and Anthropic do. That asymmetry is the most important structural fact in AI right now.

⚠ The question neither side is answering cleanly Anthropic has raised allegations that DeepSeek V4 may incorporate research from Anthropic's own work. The accusation is unresolved and contested. If substantiated, DeepSeek's pricing advantage would reflect intellectual property arbitrage rather than pure engineering efficiency. This is worth monitoring — but it does not change the current pricing reality that every business using AI infrastructure faces this week.

For MENA businesses: the AI cost conversation just changed permanently. Any AI budget built on 2025 pricing assumptions is now out of date. If your team is routing significant workloads through frontier models, the question of whether lower-cost alternatives can handle parts of those workloads without meaningful quality loss is now worth a conversation — not a dismissal. This is not a recommendation to switch. It is a prompt to know what you are paying for and why.

04 — Regional Intelligence
✓ Confirmed: Microsoft AI Diffusion Report Q1 2026, published mid-May 2026

The UAE Hit 70.1% AI Adoption. Here Is What That Number Tells You — and What It Doesn't.

The Microsoft AI Economy Institute's AI Diffusion Report for Q1 2026 placed the UAE first globally in AI adoption, with 70.1% of working-age people using AI tools. The UAE is the first economy on earth to cross the 70% threshold. The global average stands at 17.8%. Many countries remain below 10%. By that measure alone, the UAE's position is extraordinary.

✓ What 70.1% confirms The UAE's investment in AI infrastructure, education, and workforce development has produced real, measurable results in tool penetration. The government's coordinated approach — through the UAE AI Strategy, the National AI Officer network, and mandatory upskilling across government entities — is working. The number is not statistical noise.
⚠ What 70.1% does not tell you Adoption measures whether people are using AI tools. It does not measure whether those tools are changing outcomes, generating revenue, reducing costs, or improving decisions. The UAE's earlier 54% adoption rate (Stanford AI Index, April 2026) coexisted with most businesses failing to convert AI use into measurable business outcomes — as Neural Horizons AI reported in May. A higher adoption number does not resolve that gap. It makes it more urgent.

As an AI leader operating in this region, both truths need to hold at the same time. The UAE's position is genuinely impressive. It is also a foundation, not a finish line. The organisations that treat the 70.1% statistic as validation — rather than as the starting point for a harder conversation about what AI is actually changing inside the business — are the ones that will fall behind when the market recalibrates around outcomes rather than adoption.

One additional development worth noting: the UAE Cyber Security Council reported more than 800,000 cyber attacks per day in recent months and launched the UAE Cyber Factory — a sovereign AI-driven national cybersecurity initiative — in mid-May. As AI adoption rises and the regional threat landscape intensifies, the two curves are moving together. More AI in your workforce means more AI in your attack surface.

05 — AI for Humanity
✓ Confirmed: 14 May 2026

Anthropic and the Gates Foundation: $200M to Put Claude Where the Market Won't Go

On 14 May, Anthropic and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation announced a four-year, $200 million partnership to deploy Claude across global health, life sciences, education, and economic mobility programmes. The commitment includes grant funding, Claude usage credits, and direct technical support. It is led by Anthropic's Beneficial Deployments team and targets populations the commercial AI market has little structural incentive to serve.

◦ What the partnership covers Global health: screening drug and vaccine candidates for neglected diseases including polio, HPV, and eclampsia; improving forecasts for malaria and tuberculosis treatment deployment via the Gates Foundation's Institute for Disease Modeling. Education: K-12 tutoring, college advising, literacy, and numeracy tools. Agriculture: crop datasets and evaluation benchmarks for smallholder farmers. Economic mobility: portable skills records. Target population: approximately 4.6 billion people in low- and middle-income countries lacking access to essential health services.

This matters for MENA leaders beyond the headline. The partnership signals something important about Anthropic's positioning: the company with the world's highest valuation is explicitly investing in the use cases that do not generate direct revenue. That is either a genuine values commitment or a long-term market-building strategy — probably both. Either way, the pattern of deploying AI in data-constrained, resource-limited environments and building from-scratch datasets is exactly the challenge every company faces when deploying AI in a new domain. Watch how this partnership executes. It will teach the industry things about real-world AI deployment that controlled enterprise environments cannot.
