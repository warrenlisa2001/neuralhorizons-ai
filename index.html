The Horizon

We Are Not Planning for AI.
We're Planning for AGI.

Artificial General Intelligence — systems that can reason, plan, and execute across domains without human direction — is no longer a distant theoretical prospect. The leading AI labs are publishing AGI timelines of 2–5 years. Enterprise-grade agentic systems that operate autonomously across complex workflows are available today.

Every strategy we build accounts for this horizon. We don't design for the AI landscape of 2023 — we design for the capability environment of 2026 and beyond. That means your roadmap includes the transition from supervised AI use to autonomous AI operation, with the governance structures and organisational design to match.

The organisations that will win are the ones that build their AI infrastructure now — so that when AGI-adjacent capabilities become commercially accessible, they are already positioned to absorb and deploy them, rather than scrambling to catch up.

Agentic AI — Now

Autonomous agents that execute multi-step tasks, make decisions, and operate across systems without human involvement. Deployable today.

Narrow AGI — Emerging

Domain-specific systems that match or exceed human expert performance. Already live in legal research, financial analysis, and software development.

General AGI — The Horizon

Cross-domain reasoning, planning, and execution without human direction. The organisations building now will be the ones who benefit when it arrives.

Your Position

We assess where your organisation sits on this spectrum and design the roadmap that moves you forward — at a pace and risk level your business can sustain.
